Research

Research Statement

My research focuses on foundational issues in metaphysics, logic, and the philosophy of language. I am particularly interested in questions concerning grounding, logical form, metaphysical explanation, and the structure of semantic content. My work engages both contemporary analytic traditions and historical sources, drawing connections between logic, ontology, and philosophical methodology.

Dissertation

I am currently developing a dissertation that explores the relationship between logical form and metaphysical structure, with an emphasis on the role of grounding in explaining formal representation. My aim is to clarify how logical and metaphysical frameworks intersect in theories of truth, content, and necessity.

Areas of Interest

  • Metaphysics (especially grounding, dependence, identity)
  • Philosophy of Language (reference, metasemantics)
  • Philosophical Logic (formal theories of truth, logical form)
  • History of Analytic Philosophy
